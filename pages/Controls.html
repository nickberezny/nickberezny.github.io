Robustness Framework for Interaction Control

Unified Interaction Control (UIC)
UIC is a generalization of UIAC and IM-IC. It alters a typical Admittance controller with an inner-loop gain weighting and a virtual state reset to interpolate across the IC/AC spectrum. This method also explains the effect of the reset, which is also found in UIAC and IM-IC. Applications with Unavoidable Force Sensor Error
Here I applied the robustness perspective on interaction control for devices with inherent force sensor errors. Examples could include robot grasping, exoskeletons, though we do a case study with low-cost force sensors.
Using UIC, optimal performance can be achieved by trading-off robustness. The optimal level of interpolation is ultimately dependent on the physical impedance of the robot and the accuracy of the dynamic model.
Contact Stability with Industrial Robots
Here I applied interpolation to the problem of contact stability in industrial robots. To the extent that contact oscillations are caused by force sensor error (specifically, delay), they can be mitigated by interpolating to inject some robustness to this error. Experiments were carried out on a 6-DOF deburring robot.
High Impedance Haptics
Application of the IC/AC spectrum to high-impedance haptic devices. Some haptic devices must be high-impedance so that they can handle larger forces (such as lower-limb robot interaction). However, these devices must also be able to render lower impedances to facilitate human-robot interaction. We show how the IC/AC spectrum translates into a trade-off between the transparency and the stability/passivity of the system. Interapolation can allow for optimization across the trade-off.
